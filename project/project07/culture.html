Roots

Culturally Fencing changed with the times. Historically, fencing started with the rapier and was later changed to the foil because of a fashion design. Epee was implemented as a more brutal realistic dueling weapon. Finally, the sabre was introduced by the Hungarians that wanted something closer to battlefield use. How did the world adapt these weapons? The short answer, colonialism. These weapons were adopted and implemented around the world. However, in those same places that adopted the sport were also places that had been European colonies for some time. For example, the Koreans had just started adopting sabre and epee fencing schools just before 1904 when the Japo-Russo war started. On the other side of the world The United States was always apart of fencing. Fencing was brought over with the original settlers and was practiced in the States even after the American Revolution. A different perspective on this was that European warfare was more advance than any other country at the time. This would often lead to foreign nations importing soldiers and trainers from Europe to train their army. This is what lead to many armies being armed with sabres rather than their tradition swords. This can be seen in both Japan and China.

How Did Culture Tie Into The Sport?

Now that Fencing or even the weapons for fencing were now dispersed across the globe, a universal standard was hard to come by. The French did not agree with most other counties regarding target area and game courtesies. This also became a problem with the Italians with the lengths of the blades.
